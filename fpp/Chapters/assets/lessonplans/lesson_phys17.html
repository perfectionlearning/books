Preview

Activate Prior Knowledge/Build Background

Before introducing physics terms presented in this chapter, review the following physics concepts from previous chapters: electric charges, velocity, vector, charge, speed, direction, force, centripetal force, Newton’s second law, accelerate, conventional current, torque, equilibrium, inertia, distance, conductor, circuit, emf, flux, magnitude, and electric field. Write the terms on the board and ask volunteers to come to the board to write a definition or an example or draw a sketch that demonstrates the physics meaning of the word. If students can’t remember the physics meaning of any terms, begin with the common meaning of a term. Then guide students to relate the common meaning to the physics meaning.

Beginning During classroom discussions, partner Beginning students with Advanced students, if possible. Encourage partners to share their thoughts before they respond or enter a classroom discussion. If grouping is not possible, work with students individually. Allow wait time for students to process their thoughts.

Intermediate Group Intermediate students with Advanced High students. Allow wait time for students to gather their thoughts. Encourage them to discuss their thoughts with their small groups before sharing with the class.

Advanced Allow wait time for students to gather their thoughts. Encourage partner discussion. As these students help their Beginning-level partners, they will be improving their own language skills at the same time they lend language support to their partners.

Advanced High Allow wait time for students to gather their thoughts and discuss terms within their small groups.

Present

Teach Academic Vocabulary

Explain, act out, and define the academic vocabulary your students will need to master to understand this chapter. Use strategies such as those suggested below for each word. Then have the students complete a vocabulary card for each term. A printable Vocabulary Card template can be found in the Student Activities.

Beginning/Intermediate: Pronounce each word and have students repeat after you. Then use realia; simple, accessible language; and/or gestures to help students understand. For example, for the term electromagnetic induction break the term into smaller parts. For electro: What word does electro sound like? Point out a chord that is attached to an electric outlet, if necessary. For magnetic: What is a magnet? What does it do? For induction, share the following sentence: “I was inducted into the National Honor Society.” What does inducted mean?

Advanced/Advanced High: See Beginning/Intermediate. Write the meaning of electromagnetic induction on the board: “the potential difference created by motion of a conductor through a magnetic field.” Ask students to state the meaning in their own words. Record students’ definitions on the board, correcting and explaining them as necessary.

Teach Key Vocabulary

Make sure to preview key vocabulary that might impair understanding. Use strategies such as the following.

Beginning/Intermediate: Discuss the common meaning of the key vocabulary. For example, ask students what the words magnetic field commonly mean (having to do with magnets; an empty space usually grass covered).

Beginning/Intermediate/Advanced/Advanced High: Give students the physics meaning of the key vocabulary. For example, magnetic field is the region where a magnet applies force. Then ask students to relate the common meanings of key words to the physics meanings.

Use the 2-Column Notes graphic organizer in the Student Activities and suggest that students record the key vocabulary in the left column and its cognate in the right column. Ask students to keep completed 2-Column Notes in a physics notebook for reference.

Teach Major Concepts

Use strategies that represent different modalities to support second language learners as they interact with the content.

• Whiteboards Pair Beginning students with Advanced students and Intermediate with Advanced High students to study the whiteboards that introduce new concepts. Encourage the partners to begin by listening to the oral explanation that accompanies all whiteboards as they first view the new concept, providing visual and auditory support. Then encourage partners to go step-by-step through the whiteboards again with the more advanced student pointing out and reading the explanation text. Encourage the Beginning and Intermediate students to question their partners for clarification. Suggest that all students use the T-Chart Notes graphic organizer for recording formulas and summarizing concepts in their native language or English, depending on their language proficiency. Encourage students to keep their notes in a physics notebook for handy reference.

• Videos Pair Beginning students with Advanced students and Intermediate with Advanced High students to view videos that introduce academic vocabulary and key concepts. Encourage discussion between partners and have them use the T-Chart Notes graphic organizer from the Student Activities for recording formulas and summarizing concepts in their native language or English, depending on their language proficiency. Encourage students to discuss and review their notes to help them prepare for exams.

• Vocabulary Before students complete each section, review any pertinent vocabulary. Write the unknown words on the board and ask volunteers to draw a sketch or write a synonym or definition for each. Although many of the sections have images, students may not know which part of the image refers to the vocabulary, so it is helpful to have them preview the vocabulary. Have students use a copy of the Chapter 17 Vocabulary reproducible in the Student Activities to record cognates, definitions in their native language, or diagrams to help them understand and remember.

Pertinent Vocabulary: Topic 17.1: bar magnet, iron, iron filings, compass; Topic 17.2: equator; Topic 17.3: right hand, thumbs; Topic 17.5: needles, wraps around; Topic 17.7: horseshoe, rotor, coil; Topic 17.9: battery, light bulb; Topic 17.10: surface area; Topic 17.14: tube, fired, deflected; Topic 17.15: satellite; Topic 17.16: strip of gold; Topic 17.17: segment of wire, electric motor, axis, rotate, loop, pivot rod; Topic 17.26: crank, leads. Remind students to file their Chapter 17 Vocabulary sheets in their physics notebooks.

• Language of Math/Science Record the following terms on the board: mass, animation, perpendicular, angle, constant, uniform, molecule, atom, radius, initial, electrons, proportional, clockwise, intersects, downward, upward, external, internal, rectangle, horizontal, and ratio. Read through the list slowly as students repeat. Then read each term again, and ask students to give a thumbs-up or thumbs-down based on whether they are familiar with each word. Finally, pair Beginning and Intermediate students with Advanced or Advanced/High students to group the words by how they relate to each other. For example, perpendicular, downward, upward, external, internal, and horizontal all show direction, so these words could go together. Explain to students that there are many correct groupings for the words, but students will need to be ready to explain their reasoning for the groupings they choose. When pairs complete their groupings, have a class sharing and discussion of the meanings of each term, the groupings, and the reasoning for each.

Practice

Support second language learners as they practice new concepts by interfacing with Kinetic Books interactive content.

1.) Interactive Activities Pair second language students with native language speakers for these “hands-on” activities. Ask the native language speakers to read the directions aloud and then model how to perform the activity. Encourage them to use metacognition, or think-alouds, as they play, explaining what they are doing and why as their partners observe. Encourage second language students to request clarification and, when they are ready, to enjoy the interactive activity themselves. Suggest that students complete a Think, Pair, Share template together once they complete the activity. The template can be found in the Student Activities. Then provide time for both partners to share with the group what they have learned from the interactive activity.

2.) Quickchecks Project Quickchecks on a screen or whiteboard for all ELL students. Read through each problem as ELL students solve them independently by writing their answers on paper. Then have students enter their answers and check, encouraging them to use the Step function for additional help.

3.) Practice Chapter Test Project the practice chapter test on a screen or whiteboard and read the problems as second language students write their answers independently. Then pair Beginning students with Advanced students and Intermediate with Advanced High students to view and check answers and review specific sections. Encourage them to discuss concepts as they review sections. Suggest they refer to their physics notebooks.

4.) Summary Videos Pair Beginning students with Advanced students and Intermediate with Advanced High students to view videos that summarize key concepts. Encourage discussion between partners. Have them refer to their physics notebooks as they view the videos and review and discuss the concepts.
