Thermal Energy Safety

Do not touch heated surfaces without proper protection, such as insulated gloves.

Let hot liquids, solids, or gases cool down before transferring them or cleaning them up.

Do not expose any part of your body to steam.

Demonstration 1: Observing the Transfer of Thermal Energy

In Exercise 1, we want you to consider the transfer of thermal energy using a variety of materials in your classroom.

Question(s) for Students

Which methods of thermal energy transfer can be observed in solids, liquids, and gases?

Steps in the Demonstration


Create a Data Collection Chart

  1. Column 1: Name of object or substance observed
  2. Column 2: Observation location (for example, a sunny window)
  3. Column 3: Was thermal energy transferred? Yes/No
  4. Columns 4-6: Labeled Convection, Conduction, and Radiation, respectively
  5. Place the following in the sunlight:
    1. Wooden craft stick
    2. Glass rod
    3. Black construction paper
    4. Piece of aluminum foil
    5. Small balloon filled with air (Donâ€™t forget to anchor this in position; you might tie a string onto the knotted end of the balloon and either use a weight at the other end of the string or tie it in place.)
    6. Petri dish with water
    7. Petri dish with water placed on top of black construction paper
    8. Petri dish with water placed on top of a piece of aluminum foil
    9. Using the thermometer, measure the temperature of the air closest to the sunlit window and compare it to the air temperature in the rest of the classroom.
  6. Place the same items from step 5 away from the sunlit window and in front of a fan.
  7. Place the following in a dish pan of very warm water:
    1. Wooden craft stick
    2. Glass rod
    3. Metal spoon
    4. Plastic spoon
    5. Air-filled balloon
    6. Beaker with ice
    7. Beaker with water
    8. Using the thermometer, measure the temperature of the air closest to the container of very warm water and compare it to the air temperature in the rest of the classroom.
  8. Put on safety goggles. Then place the following on a hot plate and turn on the heat to medium. As another option, use a ring stand, ring clamps, clamps, and 90-degree rod clamps to hold a 4-inch ring and a ring stand screen over a Bunsen burner. Place the beakers on the ring stand screen.
    1. Beaker of tap water
    2. Beaker of ice
    3. When you have completed your observations, turn off the heat and allow the containers to cool. Remove using a thermal glove and place on a heat resistant surface or hot pad until completely cool.
  9. Place the following in a microwave oven in a microwave-safe container for 20 to 30 seconds:
    1. water
    2. ice
    3. When you have completed your observations, remove the items using a thermal glove and place on a heat resistant surface or hot pad until completely cool.
